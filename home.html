Welcome to Salima's website!

I am a PhD candidate at TEILab (Taiwan Evolutionary Intelligence Laboratory) advised by Dr. Tian-Li Yu in Electrical Engineering at NTU (National Taiwan University).

Before joining TEILab, I did my Master's degree in Statistics at NTU and my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics Sciences at NCCU (National Chengchi University, Taiwan). My research focuses on developing theoretical support for model-based genetic algorithms, aiming to bridge the gap between theoretical understanding and practical applications of these algorithms in solving complex optimization problems.

From February to August 2023, I interned at USDA (United States Department of Agriculture), contributing to the development of a Linkout plugin for JBrowse 2 and a machine learning model for predicting images in insect databases using single nucleotide polymorphisms. Recently, I interned at the NII (National Institute of Informatics, Japan) from February to April 2024, focusing on generating molecular graphs through model-based genetic algorithms.

Subsequently, I will be a visiting student at MIT (Massachusetts Institute of Technology, USA) from June 2024 to May 2025, where I will work on advancing fully interpretable simulators for pre-mRNA splicing. I'd love to have academic discussions with anyone, and any feedback is welcome. Please contact me via salimachchang@gmail.com.
